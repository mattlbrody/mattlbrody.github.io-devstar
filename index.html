Hiring

For new developers, it can be scary trying to get a job. You don't know what kinds of questions their going to ask and you want to look like you know what you're doing. The hiring part of the course will help alleviate your fears and get you going in the right direction.

Your Portfolio Website

The first thing companies look at when they're hiring new front-end developers is their portfolio. It's represents all the work you've completed and gives them a good idea of what you know. During the course, you'll build a great looking portfolio with a ton of great projects you'll be proud to show off.

Resume, Cover Letter, and LinkedIn

After the portfolio, the next thing they'll look at is who you are and what your background is. You'll learn how to craft your story and enhance your online presence to boost the total number of interviews you get.

Interview Preparation

Interviews are the scariest part of applying for new jobs, especially in a field you've never worked before. During the course, you'll learn the most common interview questions for front-end developers along with how to best answer them.
